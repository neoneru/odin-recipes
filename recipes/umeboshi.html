Umeboshi Udon

Description

The citric acid in the sour plum has a stimulating effect on the appetite. This is a great recipe for when you don't feel like eating.

Ingredients

  • 150g dried udon noodles
  • 4 umeboshi (pickled plum>
  • 1 piece myoga ginger
  • Tororo-kombu, as desired
  • Green yuzu peel, as desired
  • White sesame seeds, as desired
  • 3 tbsp sake
  • A dash of soy sauce
  • 500ml water

Method

  1. Heat 3 tablespoons of sake in a pot to burn off the alcohol. Add water and umeboshi and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat and allow it to cool. Transfer to a glass, enamel or porcelain jar and refrigerate for at least half a day.
  2. Cut the myoga into thin shreds and soak in water for several minutes. Drain.
  3. Pour a generous amount of water into a large pot and bring to a boil. Add the udon noodles and boil according to the instructions on the package. After draining and rinsing under running water, chill in ice water. Drain thoroughly and plate up.
  4. Take the umeboshi out of the dashi. Remove the umeboshi pits and tear up the fruit. Season the dashi with soy sauce.
  5. Pour the dashi over the udon in a circular motion and top with the umeboshi fruit, myoga and tororo-kombu. Sprinkle with grated green yuzu peel and white sesame seed.
